## What is the idea behind this blog?

I learn best when I have to explain an idea clearly. Kevin's Lessons Learned is
where I turn questions, experiments, mistakes, and practical experience into
durable notes that I can revisit and other people can use.

This is learning in public, not a claim to have the final word. A useful post
should show what changed in my understanding, explain the reasoning, and leave
the reader with something they can apply.

## How does an idea become a post?

Most topics begin as a GitHub Issue. I use it to capture the question, collect
sources, test the idea against real experience, and decide whether there is a
lesson worth sharing. The post comes later, once I can explain the subject
clearly and distinguish evidence from opinion or hindsight.

## What topics do you write about?

The subjects follow what I am actively learning, especially machine learning,
AI systems, data quality, evaluation, production engineering, and the human
processes around them. Posts may be technical deep dives, project
retrospectives, tutorials, or shorter notes about a lesson worth remembering.

## What if I find an error or want to discuss a post?

Please [open an issue in the GitHub repository](https://github.com/xiaochuan-du/kevin-lessons-learned/issues/new).
Corrections, counterexamples, missing references, and thoughtful disagreements
are all welcome. An issue keeps the discussion connected to the blog and makes
the outcome useful to future readers.

## Do you update older posts?

Yes. This is a living notebook, so I may revise a post when I learn something
new, find a clearer explanation, or receive a useful correction. Git history
keeps those changes visible.

## Which technical blogs do you recommend?

If you follow only one, start with [Lil'Log by Lilian Weng](https://lilianweng.github.io/).
It is my strongest recommendation and the clearest inspiration for this site:
careful long-form learning notes, strong diagrams, useful references, and ideas
explained deeply enough to remain valuable.

I also love and regularly learn from:

- [Jeremy Jordan](https://www.jeremyjordan.me/) — clear explanations of machine
  learning concepts and the systems around them.
- [Chip Huyen](https://huyenchip.com/blog/) — practical writing about AI system
  design and bringing machine learning into production.
- [Sebastian Raschka](https://sebastianraschka.com/blog/) — detailed,
  implementation-oriented explanations of machine learning and modern LLMs.
- [Eugene Yan](https://eugeneyan.com/) — grounded lessons on applied AI,
  recommendation systems, product development, and engineering.

The [Lil'Log FAQ](https://lilianweng.github.io/faq/) also inspired the simple
question-and-answer structure of this page. The words and opinions here are my
own.
